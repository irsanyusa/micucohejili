Frida Kahlo (Painter) - On This Day

Full Name: Magdalena Carmen Frida Kahlo y Calderón
Profession: Painter

Biography: Frida Kahlo began painting in 1925 while recovering from a serious traffic accident. Her work revolved around her own identity, and in particular the physical pain she suffered after her accident and her tempestuous relationship with fellow Mexican artist Diego Rivera.

Frida met Diego Rivera through the Mexican Communist Party, which they were both members of. They married in 1929. Ex-martial affairs marked their marriage and led to their divorce in 1939 before they remarried again the next year.

Kahlo received some recognition during her lifetime, French surrealist artist André Breton arranged her first solo exhibition at the Julien Levy Gallery in New York in 1938.

However true international fame did not come until after Kahlo's death, when in the late 1970s she was acclaimed as a feminist and for her explorations of Mexican identity.

Born: July 6, 1907
Birthplace: Coyoacán, Mexico City, Distrito Federal, Mexico

Generation: Greatest Generation
Chinese Zodiac: Goat/Sheep
Star Sign: Cancer

Died: July 13, 1954 (aged 47)
Cause of Death: Pulmonary embolism
